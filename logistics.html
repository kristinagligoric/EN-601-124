Format Overview

This course meets twice per week for 75 minutes and combines lectures, quizzes, reflections, a midterm, and structured active-learning discussions. The goal is to balance instruction with critical engagement with readings and peer perspectives, with regular opportunities for reflection and feedback.

Class Structure and Weekly Flow

  • Meeting 1 – Monday: Lecture and quiz
    • Lecture & in-class activity (60 min): Instructor-led lecture and interactive activity focused on core concepts for the week.
    • In-class quiz (15 min): A short quiz based on the previous week’s lecture and assigned readings.
    • Attendance: Attendance is mandatory.
  • Weekly reflection – due Tuesday EOD
    • Students submit a short reflection via Canvas by Tuesday end of day, responding to the lecture and readings. These reflections may inform the discussion prompts used later in the week.
  • Midterm
    • There will be one midterm exam, structured similarly to the weekly quizzes and reflections, focused on conceptual understanding and critical engagement with course material.
  • Meeting 2 – Wednesday: Active learning and guided discussion
    • Guided discussion of readings: The class works through discussion prompts and/or debates based on the assigned papers. Prompts may draw on themes from student reflections.
    • Small-group discussions: Students are split into groups for facilitated discussion.
    • Report-back: For each prompt, one group member reports key takeaways to the full class.
    • Preview of next week: The final minutes are used to introduce the readings and themes for the following week.
    • Attendance: Attendance is mandatory.

Absences

Each student has up to three free cards that may be used to miss class without penalty. Each student also has up to three free cards that may be used to miss an assignment (reflection or quiz) without penalty. Students are expected to contact the instructor in advance to use the free cards. No explanation is needed.
