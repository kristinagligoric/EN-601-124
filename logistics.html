Format Overview

This course meets twice per week and combines lectures, quizzes, reflections, a midterm, and structured active-learning discussions. The goal is to balance instruction with critical engagement with readings and peer perspectives, with regular opportunities for reflection and feedback.

Class Structure and Weekly Flow

  • Meeting 1 – Monday: Lecture and quiz
    • Lecture & in-class activity (60 min): Instructor-led lecture and interactive activity focused on core concepts for the week.
    • In-class quiz (15 min): A short quiz based on the previous week’s lecture and assigned readings. There will be six quizzes over the semester.
    • Attendance: Attendance is mandatory.
  • Weekly reflection – due Tuesday EOD
    • Students submit a short reflection via Canvas by Tuesday end of day, responding to the lecture and readings. These reflections may inform the discussion prompts used later in the week. They will be graded for submission. There will be ten reading reflections in total.
  • Meeting 2 – Wednesday: Active learning and guided discussion
    • Guided discussion of readings: The class works through discussion prompts and/or debates based on the assigned papers. Prompts may draw on themes from student reflections.
    • Small-group discussions: Students are split into groups for facilitated discussion.
    • Report-back: For each prompt, one group member reports key takeaways to the full class.
    • Preview of next week: The final minutes are used to introduce the readings and themes for the following week.
    • Attendance: Attendance and active participation are mandatory.
  • Midterm exam
    • There will be one midterm exam, structured similarly to the weekly quizzes and guided reflections, focused on conceptual understanding and critical engagement with course material.

Course Deliverables and Grade Breakdown

  • Weekly in-class attendance and active participation: 10%
  • Reading reflection submissions (submission only): 20%
  • Short in-class quizzes: 15%
  • In-class midterm: 25%
  • Semester-long group research project: 30%
    • Project proposal: 5%
    • Project milestone: 5%
    • Group project report: 10%
    • Group project presentation: 10%

Absences

Each student has up to three free cards that may be used to miss an entire class, including any assignment for that day, the quiz (for Monday sessions) or the reflection (for Wednesday sessions), without penalty. Students are expected to notify the instructor in advance when using a free card. No explanation is required.

Late submissions

Late submissions are not allowed.

Why this workload?

The workload in this course is designed to support continued engagement with complex ethical issues. Short weekly reflections and periodic low-stakes quizzes are intended to encourage preparation and ongoing reflection rather than high-pressure performance. These assignments are graded only for completion, to promote consistency and discussion. Deeper evaluation is concentrated in the midterm and the group project.
